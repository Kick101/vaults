__Definition:__ Threat Modeling is a structured process to identify and enumerate potential threats and vulnerabilities of a system (Computer, Network, Application).
- It is one of the ways to approach for `risk management`  which mainly focuses on analyzing the network security and application security against vulnerabilities, threats lack of defense and prioritize security mitigations. 

__Risk Management:__ It is the potential for loss, damage, destruction of assets or data caused by a cyber threat. What is the worst can happen with existing threats is the risk management.
__Threat Modeling:__ What are threats that exist in the system is the threat modeling.
<br>
Basic layman *Threat Modeling Process:*
1. Identify Security Objectives
	- Identify what security measures are taken, what are not taken, what are not applied and what must be taken
2. Application Overview
	- Working, purpose of the application, what things can be done etc
	- Stats of the Application: no.of users etc
3. Decompose Application
	- Divide the application modules and identify their technologies
4. Identify Threats
	- A threat is a potential negative action or event facilitated by a vulnerability
	- Identify what elements have the potential in causing problems
	- Threats like: Objects, substances, humans etc. Anything which can cause loss to system such as software attacks, theft of intellectual property, identity theft, theft of equipment, information, sabotage and information extortion(ransomware).
5. Identify Vulnerabilities
	- Vulnerability is a weakness or flaw which can be exploited by a threat actor.
	- Identify potential vulnerabilities that exists in the system.

<br>
There are several Cyber Threat Modeling methodologies:

- __STRIDE__ (Spoofing, Tampering, Repudiation, Information Disclosure, Denial of Service, Elevation of Privilege)
- __VAST__ (Visual, Agile and Simple Threat)
- __OCTAVE__ (Operationally Critical Threat, Asset and Vulnerability Evaluation)
- __PASTA__ (Stages of Process for Attack Simulation and Threat Analysis)
- __DREAD__ (Damage, Reproducibility, Exploitation, Affected users, Discoverability)
- __TRIKE__ ()
